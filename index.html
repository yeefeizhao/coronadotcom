Symptoms of COVID-19

The severity of symptoms may be different from person to person and may not start to appear for 2-14 days. Common symptoms include

Fever or chills
Cough
Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
Fatigue
Muscle or body aches
Headache
New loss of taste or smell
Sore throat
Congestion or runny nose
Nausea or vomiting
Diarrhea

Prevention

COVID-19 is mainly spread between two people of close contact or within 6 feet of each other through respiratory droplets. Some people may not show signs of having contracted the disease. This makes it very dangerous, as no one knows who is ill and who is healthy. The virus can also be contracted from touching surfaces that have the virus on it. To protect yourself and others around you, maintain good distance (at least 6 feet), wear a mask to cover nose and mouth (when around others), wear gloves, wash your hands frequently, don't touch your face, and clean surfaces regularly.

What to Do When You Are Sick

If you have contracted COVID-19, stay home and stay away from public areas, stay hydrated and contact your doctor. While sick, continue to practice COVID-19 prevention measures (i.e. wearing a mask, wearing gloves, etc). Monitor your symptoms and watch for emergency warning signs, which include:

Trouble breathing
Persistent pain or pressure in the chest
New confusion
Inability to wake or stay awake
Bluish lips or face
If these symptoms or other severe symptoms arise, call 911 or call ahead to a local emergency facility as soon as possible.
